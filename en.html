About

I'm an undergraduate at the University of Waterloo, studying Combinatorics & Optimization with a Statistics minor. I came to machine learning through math — drawn to clean structure and curious about why complex systems behave the way they do.

My research interests sit at the intersection of reinforcement learning, world models, and mathematical optimization — broadly, how agents learn to predict and control the dynamics of their environments, and how training procedures stay stable as systems scale up. I also enjoy the systems side of getting research ideas to actually run end-to-end.

Outside of research I'm a regular hackathon participant and occasional open-source contributor, and a believer in evaluation discipline — clean baselines, honest metrics, and writing code other people can clone and run.
